Analysis of U.S. Strikes on Iran’s Nuclear Facilities

Introduction

On June 22, 2025, the United States escalated its involvement in the Israel-Iran conflict by launching Operation Midnight Hammer, a direct military strike on Iran’s nuclear facilities at Fordow, Natanz, and Isfahan. The operation involved six B-2 Spirit stealth bombers dropping 12 GBU-57A/B Massive Ordnance Penetrator (MOP) “bunker buster” bombs on the heavily fortified Fordow site and U.S. Navy submarines firing 30 Tomahawk Land Attack Missiles (TLAMs) at Natanz and Isfahan. President Donald Trump hailed the strikes as a “spectacular military success,” claiming Iran’s nuclear enrichment capabilities were “completely obliterated.” However, U.S. and Israeli officials offered more cautious assessments, noting severe damage but not total destruction. The strikes drew condemnation from Hamas and prompted calls for diplomacy from Colombia and Mexico. This blog analyzes the military operation, its strategic impact, regional reactions, and diplomatic implications, drawing on available reports.

U.S. Military Operation: Operation Midnight Hammer

Execution and Targets

The U.S. strikes, conducted under the codename Operation Midnight Hammer, targeted three key Iranian nuclear facilities:

  • Fordow Uranium Enrichment Plant: Located 80–90 meters underground near Qom, Fordow is Iran’s most fortified nuclear site, designed to withstand conventional attacks. Six B-2 Spirit stealth bombers dropped 12 GBU-57A/B MOPs, each weighing 30,000 pounds, targeting ventilation shafts and centrifuge halls to disrupt uranium enrichment. Satellite imagery shows six craters along the ridge above Fordow, indicating significant surface damage, though internal damage remains unclear.
    • [](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/6/22/satellite-images-show-damage-from-us-strikes-on-irans-fordow-nuclear-site)[](https://www.washingtonpost.com/investigations/2025/06/22/iran-strikes-nuclear-site-damage-visuals/)
  • Natanz Nuclear Facility: Iran’s largest uranium enrichment complex, previously damaged by Israeli strikes on June 13, was hit by two MOPs from a B-2 bomber and TLAMs from U.S. submarines. The strikes targeted underground enrichment halls and electrical infrastructure, with satellite imagery showing new craters and damage to aboveground structures.
    • [](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2025/06/22/world/middleeast/us-iran-nuclear-sites.html)[](https://www.cnn.com/2025/06/21/middleeast/nuclear-sites-iran-us-bombs-wwk-intl)
  • Isfahan Nuclear Technology Center: A key research and conversion facility, Isfahan was struck by over two dozen TLAMs, destroying at least 18 structures, including a reactor fuel manufacturing plant and chemical laboratory. The site’s underground tunnels, potentially storing enriched uranium, may have been less affected due to their depth.
    • [](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/6/22/satellite-images-show-damage-from-us-strikes-on-irans-fordow-nuclear-site)[](https://www.washingtonpost.com/investigations/2025/06/22/iran-strikes-nuclear-site-damage-visuals/)

The operation involved 125 U.S. aircraft, including B-2 bombers, F-22 Raptors, F-35 Lightning IIs, EA-18G Growlers, and refueling tankers, flying an 18-hour mission from Whiteman Air Force Base, Missouri. Deception tactics, such as deploying decoy jets to the Pacific, ensured the B-2s entered Iranian airspace undetected, aided by prior Israeli strikes that degraded Iran’s air defenses.

[](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cew0x7159edo)[](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/cvg9r4q99g4o)

Strategic Objectives

The U.S. aimed to:

  1. Destroy or severely impair Iran’s nuclear enrichment capabilities, particularly at Fordow, which could produce weapons-grade uranium in weeks.
  2. Support Israel’s campaign to prevent Iran from developing nuclear weapons, following Israel’s inability to fully neutralize Fordow.
  3. Signal U.S. resolve to counter Iran’s nuclear ambitions and pressure Tehran into diplomatic concessions.

The use of GBU-57 MOPs, designed to penetrate up to 60 meters of earth or 18 meters of concrete, marked their first combat deployment, underscoring the operation’s significance. The TLAMs targeted aboveground infrastructure to maximize disruption.

[](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2025/06/20/world/middleeast/iran-nuclear-fordo-bunker-buster-bomb.html)[](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cew0x7159edo)

Assessments of Damage and Effectiveness

President Trump claimed the strikes “completely obliterated” Iran’s nuclear facilities, but U.S. and Israeli officials provided more measured assessments:

  • Fordow: U.S. officials reported that the 12 MOPs likely damaged centrifuge halls and ventilation systems, setting back enrichment capabilities by up to two years. However, Iran’s pre-strike evacuation of nuclear materials and sealing of tunnel entrances may have mitigated internal damage.
    • [](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/national-security/new-us-assessment-finds-american-strikes-destroyed-only-one-three-iran-rcna218761)[](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/jun/22/how-effective-was-the-us-attack-on-irans-nuclear-sites-a-visual-guide)
  • Natanz: The strikes destroyed critical electrical infrastructure and possibly damaged underground centrifuges, building on prior Israeli attacks that had already crippled 75% of the facility.
    • [](https://theaviationist.com/2025/06/22/breaking-b-2-spirit-bombers-struck-iranian-nuclear-sites/)[](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2025/06/22/world/middleeast/us-iran-nuclear-sites.html)
  • Isfahan: TLAMs caused extensive damage to aboveground structures, but deeper tunnels likely protected some enriched uranium stocks. Experts note that Isfahan’s role in uranium conversion makes its destruction significant but not decisive.
    • [](https://www.npr.org/2025/06/22/nx-s1-5441734/satellites-show-damage-iran-nuclear-program-not-destroyed-experts-say)[](https://www.washingtonpost.com/investigations/2025/06/22/iran-strikes-nuclear-site-damage-visuals/)

Satellite imagery confirms severe surface damage at all three sites, but Iran’s claims of evacuating uranium and the resilience of underground facilities suggest the nuclear program was not entirely eliminated. The International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) reported no off-site radiation, indicating no major radioactive releases.

[](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/6/22/satellite-images-show-damage-from-us-strikes-on-irans-fordow-nuclear-site)[](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/jun/22/how-effective-was-the-us-attack-on-irans-nuclear-sites-a-visual-guide)

Hamas Condemnation

Hamas, a key Iranian ally, condemned the U.S. strikes as a “violation of international law” and a “dangerous escalation,” reflecting Iran’s influence over the group. This response highlights:

  • Iran’s Regional Network: Hamas’s statement aligns with Iran’s narrative, emphasizing the broader Axis of Resistance, including Hezbollah and the Houthis, which may escalate attacks in response.
  • Political Messaging: By framing the strikes as illegal, Hamas seeks to rally anti-U.S. and anti-Israel sentiment, potentially mobilizing support in Gaza and beyond.
  • Limited Direct Impact: Hamas’s condemnation is rhetorical, as its military capabilities are constrained by ongoing Israeli operations in Gaza, but it underscores the conflict’s regional ramifications.

Hamas’s reaction amplifies Iran’s diplomatic efforts to portray the U.S. and Israel as aggressors, complicating international mediation.

Diplomatic Push by Colombia and Mexico

Colombia and Mexico issued joint statements urging a return to negotiations to resolve the crisis, emphasizing peaceful coexistence. Their stance reflects:

  • Neutral Advocacy: As non-aligned nations, Colombia and Mexico aim to de-escalate tensions, advocating for dialogue to prevent a broader regional conflict.
  • Support for International Norms: Their call aligns with the IAEA’s warnings about the risks of bombing nuclear sites, emphasizing adherence to the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty (NPT).
    • [](https://www.twz.com/news-features/u-s-has-attacked-irans-nuclear-facilities)
  • Limited Influence: While their diplomatic push signals global concern, their lack of direct leverage over the U.S., Israel, or Iran limits their impact on the conflict.

This diplomatic effort underscores the international community’s anxiety about escalation but faces challenges given Iran’s distrust and U.S.-Israeli military momentum.

Analysis: Tactical Success, Strategic Uncertainty

Operation Midnight Hammer represents a historic U.S. military operation, leveraging the B-2’s unique capability to deploy GBU-57 MOPs and achieving significant damage to Iran’s nuclear infrastructure. The strikes’ key strengths include:

  • Precision and Scale: The use of 125 aircraft and 75 precision-guided weapons, including 14 MOPs, demonstrates unparalleled U.S. military capability. The operation’s success in avoiding Iranian defenses highlights effective coordination with Israel.
    • [](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/cvg9r4q99g4o)
  • Strategic Impact: The damage to Fordow, Natanz, and Isfahan sets back Iran’s nuclear program by potentially years, particularly at Fordow, which U.S. officials claim was “taken off the table.”
    • [](https://theaviationist.com/2025/06/22/breaking-b-2-spirit-bombers-struck-iranian-nuclear-sites/)[](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/national-security/new-us-assessment-finds-american-strikes-destroyed-only-one-three-iran-rcna218761)

However, several factors temper the operation’s long-term effectiveness:

  • Incomplete Destruction: Iran’s evacuation of uranium and sealing of tunnels at Fordow and Isfahan likely preserved some nuclear material. Natanz’s underground facilities may also remain partially operational.
    • [](https://www.npr.org/2025/06/22/nx-s1-5441734/satellites-show-damage-iran-nuclear-program-not-destroyed-experts-say)[](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/jun/22/how-effective-was-the-us-attack-on-irans-nuclear-sites-a-visual-guide)
  • Iran’s Resilience: Iran’s claims of a secure, undisclosed enrichment site and its history of rebuilding after attacks (e.g., Stuxnet) suggest the nuclear program may recover.
    • [](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/jun/22/how-effective-was-the-us-attack-on-irans-nuclear-sites-a-visual-guide)
  • Diplomatic Fallout: The strikes, condemned by Iran and Hamas as violations of international law, have strained nuclear negotiations. Iran’s threats to target U.S. bases and close the Strait of Hormuz raise the risk of retaliation.
    • [](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2025/06/21/world/iran-israel-trump)
  • Regional Escalation: Hamas’s condemnation and potential proxy attacks by Iran’s allies could widen the conflict, destabilizing the Middle East and impacting oil markets, with Brent crude prices already rising to $76.37 per barrel.

The cautious assessments by U.S. and Israeli officials, contrasted with Trump’s bold claims, suggest a gap between public rhetoric and operational reality. The operation’s success hinges on whether Iran can reconstitute its nuclear program and how it responds militarily and diplomatically.

Conclusion

The U.S. strikes on Iran’s Fordow, Natanz, and Isfahan nuclear facilities on June 22, 2025, mark a significant escalation in the Israel-Iran conflict, showcasing U.S. military prowess through the historic use of GBU-57 MOPs. While the operation inflicted severe damage, Iran’s preemptive measures and resilient underground infrastructure suggest the nuclear program was not entirely eliminated. Hamas’s condemnation reflects Iran’s regional influence, while Colombia and Mexico’s diplomatic push highlights global concerns about escalation. The strikes have disrupted nuclear talks and raised the specter of Iranian retaliation, potentially through proxies or direct attacks on U.S. interests. As battle damage assessments continue, the international community faces the challenge of balancing military pressure with diplomacy to prevent a broader regional conflict and address Iran’s nuclear ambitions.
